Epoxy Mold Thermal Analysis · Draper Laboratory

Tasked with performing a creep analysis in ANSYS to assess Draper's proprietary epoxy mold's deformation over time and determine the assembly's lifespan under specific thermal conditions.

  • Determine deformation over time and predict structural lifespan

Challenges

  • Determine how long the assembly can maintain structural integrity with limited deformation
  • First time performing a creep simulation in ANSYS Workbench — learned independently through forums and documentation

Results

  • Incorporated detailed material properties including unique epoxy characteristics
  • Presented findings to task leader (numerical results confidential)

Capstone: Proton Therapy Mockup
Senior Capstone · Client: Varian Medical Systems

Proton Therapy uses precise proton beams to destroy tumor sites. Children (ages 6–13) often struggle to stay still during the 45-minute procedure. My team partnered with Varian to design a scaled-down mockup of their proton therapy room to educate and calm pediatric patients.

Objective

  • Design a functional scaled-down model of a proton therapy treatment room
  • Serve as a stress-relieving demonstration for pediatric patients and families

Design Constraints

  • Must fit within an 18-gallon box and be easy to carry
  • Fully functional moving bed, gantry, and nozzle
  • Interactive and controllable with a video game controller

Results

  • Two motors providing two degrees of freedom; ball joint enables yaw, pitch, and roll
  • One motor for gantry rotation; linear actuator for nozzle extension/retraction

Basketball Hoop FEA Analysis
ME4508 Computation & Design · ABAQUS, SolidWorks

Performed finite element analysis to determine how much deformation a basketball rim would endure when simulating an average person dunking on the hoop.

Design Constraints

  • 5,635 lb load applied to front of rim to simulate a 175 lb person dunking
  • 18-inch rim with 8-bolt hole pattern attaching to backboard

Challenges

  • Finding optimal material where hoop bends but doesn't break for safety
  • Software crashed on mesh sizes smaller than 0.05, limiting accuracy

Results

  • Carbon steel demonstrated a factor of safety of 818 — the better material
  • Results verified with hand calculations
